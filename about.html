I'm Daniel Hara, a Statistics and Data Science student at UCLA (graduating June 2026) with a minor in Data Science Engineering and a 3.7 GPA. I focus on applied machine learning, building systems that are statistically sound and actually useful in production.

I'm currently a Data Science Intern at WagerWire, a sports betting marketplace in Los Angeles. My work there spans Bayesian modeling for ambassador efficiency, R/SQL analytics pipelines, and Looker Studio dashboards, translating raw PostgreSQL data into insights that inform product and business decisions. I was extended from a summer internship to part-time during the academic year based on strong performance.

My coursework has covered the full stack of modern data science: probability theory, natural language processing, neural networks and deep learning, data mining, regression, optimization, and Monte Carlo methods. I apply these foundations directly in projects, from fine-tuning transformers to building end-to-end Dockerized ML applications.

I'm drawn to problems where data is messy, stakes are real, and the gap between a good model and a bad one actually matters. Sports analytics, finance, and consumer-facing ML applications are where that overlap tends to be sharpest.
